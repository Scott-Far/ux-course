Module 1 — The UX Mindset

Define it. Sketch it. Defend it.

Scott Faranello
📬

Check your inbox before you startA summary of all your Module 1 activity responses was sent to your email when you clicked through from Lesson 1.4. Have it open as you work through this project — your responses are the foundation of everything you are about to build.

What this project is

Throughout Module 1 you have been building a single thread — a real problem you noticed, a root cause you dug to, a "How might we" statement you wrote, and the smallest meaningful intervention you identified. This project asks you to bring all of that together into one cohesive response.

You are not building a finished product. You are being asked to think like a UX practitioner and show your work.

What you will submit

Part 1 — The problem

From Lesson 1.1, Activities 1 and 2

Describe the problem you identified in Lesson 1.1. What did you notice? What does it feel like to experience it? Then describe your reframe — the real problem underneath the symptom you first observed.

Be specific. "Apps are confusing" is not a problem statement. "The navigation on my bank's app uses internal labels that customers do not recognize, so people cannot find what they need without guessing" is a problem statement.

Check your inbox for your Lesson 1.1 activity responses.

Part 2 — The root cause

From Lesson 1.2, Activity 2

Using the three whys from Lesson 1.2, describe what is actually driving the problem. What did you find when you kept asking why? What is underneath the symptom you first noticed?

Explain the difference between what the problem looks like on the surface and what is actually causing it.

Part 3 — The "How might we"

From Lesson 1.3, Activity 2

Write your "How might we" statement from your Lesson 1.3 activity worksheet. One sentence. Specific enough to guide a design decision but open enough to allow more than one solution.

"How might we help mobile banking users find the transfer function without relying on labels they do not recognize?"

Part 4 — The solution sketch

From Lesson 1.4, Activity

Using the trim tab you identified in Lesson 1.4 as your starting point, sketch or describe your proposed solution. Show what it looks like and how it works. Label the parts. Explain why each decision addresses the root cause you defined.

It does not need to be beautiful. It needs to be clear. If you are drawing by hand, take a photo. If you are writing it out, be specific enough that someone who has never seen the problem could understand your solution from your description alone.

How you will be evaluated

I will review every submission personally. I am not looking for polished design. I am looking for evidence of the UX mindset:

  • Did you identify a real problem, not a symptom?
  • Did you dig past the surface to find the root cause?
  • Does your solution address the actual need, not just the obvious fix?
  • Can you explain why you made the decisions you made?

There is no single right answer. Every student will have observed a different problem and arrived at a different solution. What matters is the quality of your thinking, not the quality of your drawing.

The project thread — what each lesson added

Before you submit, check that your project includes work from all four lessons:
